# AI Content Transparency Manifesto

[English](#english) | [中文](#中文)

## English

### Background

AI-assisted content creation has entered everyday writing, design, publishing, and knowledge work. At the same time, low-quality mass-produced content, unclear authorship, and misleading presentation have become more common. Readers, viewers, and users increasingly face difficulty distinguishing between human-led work, AI-assisted work, and largely machine-generated output.

This project responds to that condition with a practical proposal: do not prohibit AI, but make its participation transparent.

### Core Position

AI is a tool, not a reason to abandon accountability. Human creators may use AI for drafting, organization, transformation, summarization, polishing, or ideation. However, when AI meaningfully participates in the final published content, the audience should have a clear and truthful declaration.

The purpose of this manifesto is not to divide creators into acceptable and unacceptable groups. Its purpose is to encourage a simple norm: if AI participation matters, disclose it plainly.

### Core Principles

1. Transparency first.
   When AI materially participates in content creation, the creator should disclose that participation in a visible and understandable form.
2. AI is not prohibited.
   This manifesto does not oppose the use of AI. It opposes hidden, exaggerated, or misleading presentation of AI-assisted work.
3. Human responsibility remains.
   The final publisher remains responsible for facts, judgments, values, and consequences, regardless of which tools were used.
4. Disclosure should be specific.
   A useful declaration should communicate estimated ratios and concrete roles, rather than vague wording such as "partially assisted."
5. Presentation must not mislead.
   Disclosure should not be buried, obscured, or framed in a way that causes readers to misunderstand the actual level of AI participation.

### Voluntary Declaration

This manifesto is a voluntary initiative. It is not a law, not a platform policy, and not a certification system. It does not create legal obligations, mandatory labels, or enforcement rights.

Its value depends on creator self-discipline, audience trust, and community adoption. The goal is to establish a healthier publishing habit: honest disclosure without stigma, panic, or prohibition.

### Call

We invite writers, bloggers, developers, researchers, designers, educators, and independent creators to adopt this standard where appropriate. We also invite platforms and communities to reference it as a lightweight good practice for AI-era publishing.

## 中文

### 背景

AI 辅助内容创作已经进入日常写作、设计、发布与知识生产流程。与此同时，低质量批量内容、作者身份模糊、表达方式误导等问题也在快速增加。读者、观众与用户越来越难以区分哪些内容是以人为主导、哪些内容是 AI 辅助完成、哪些内容则主要由机器生成。

本项目正是在这样的背景下提出一个务实倡议：不禁止 AI，但要求对 AI 的参与保持透明。

### 核心立场

AI 是工具，不是放弃责任的理由。创作者可以使用 AI 进行起草、整理、改写、总结、润色或灵感辅助。但当 AI 对最终发布内容形成实质性参与时，受众应当获得清晰、真实、可理解的说明。

本公约的目标，不是把创作者划分为“可接受”与“不可接受”的阵营，而是推动形成一个简单规范：只要 AI 的参与对成品有意义，就应当进行明确声明。

### 核心原则

1. 透明优先。
   当 AI 对内容创作产生实质性参与时，创作者应以可见、易懂的方式说明其参与情况。
2. 不禁止 AI。
   本公约不反对使用 AI，反对的是隐瞒使用、夸大人工属性，或用模糊表达掩盖 AI 参与程度。
3. 人类责任保留。
   无论使用何种工具，最终发布者仍应对事实、判断、价值取向与结果负责。
4. 声明应尽量具体。
   有效声明应说明大致占比与具体分工，而不是仅使用“部分辅助”“略有参与”等模糊措辞。
5. 展示不得误导。
   声明不应被折叠、埋藏、弱化，或以会让读者误判 AI 参与程度的方式呈现。

### 非强制性声明

本公约为自愿倡议，不是法律，不是平台规则，也不是认证体系。它不直接产生法律义务，不构成强制标签，也不提供任何形式的执法或处罚依据。

它的价值来自创作者的自律、读者的信任与社区的共同采用。我们的目标，是建立一种更健康的发布习惯：在不污名化 AI、不过度恐慌、也不否定技术工具的前提下，推动真实披露。

### 倡议对象

我们倡议作者、博主、开发者、研究者、设计师、教育者以及各类独立创作者，在适当场景中采用这一标准。也欢迎平台与社区将其作为 AI 时代内容发布的一种轻量最佳实践加以参考。
